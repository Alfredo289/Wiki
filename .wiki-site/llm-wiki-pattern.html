A knowledge management approach where an LLM agent reads raw sources and compiles them into structured, cross-referenced wiki pages. Distinguished from RAG by pre-compiling knowledge rather than re-deriving answers via vector search at query time.

Originated by andrej-karpathy in a February 2025 tweet. At ~100 articles / ~400K words, Karpathy reported this approach outperforms RAG for personal Q&A.

Key properties:

  • Pre-compiled - knowledge is structured once, updated incrementally
  • Compound loop - filed answers enrich future queries
  • Bias-checked - counter-arguments and data gaps on every page
  • Human-validated - explored gate prevents blind trust in AI output

Research vs Personal Thinking

YB (yb-effect-2026-commonplace-practice) draws a distinction: the LLM wiki pattern is “fantastic for research projects” where synthetic artifacts make sense, but for personal thinking, “you can’t outsource your own life experiences to AI.” This suggests the pattern works best for external knowledge compilation, while personal knowledge management needs a complementary approach built on commonplace-practice and atomic-notes.

Counter-arguments

  • Requires more upfront processing than RAG
  • May not scale as well to very large, rapidly changing corpora
  • Single-user testimonial, not benchmarked rigorously

Data gaps

  • No head-to-head comparison with modern RAG systems (e.g., GraphRAG)
  • Unclear at what scale the pre-compiled approach breaks down
  • Original Karpathy tweet/thread not yet ingested as a primary source
