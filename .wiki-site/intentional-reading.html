Intentional Reading

A deliberate approach to content consumption where reading time is structured into two phases: discovery (scanning for potential reads) and focused reading (highlighting, note-taking, connecting to existing knowledge).

Described by YB (yb-effect-2026-commonplace-practice) as a reaction to “cope consumption” - feeling productive while passively listening to podcasts or scrolling Substack. The core shift: every piece of content read should produce new atomic-notes and connections.

Pipeline

  1. Curate sources - “Who gets access to my brain?” (references @phokarlsson’s “Structure your milieu”)
  2. Scroll = search - browsing time is for finding potential reads to add to a backlog
  3. Read with intent - dedicated time for highlighting and note-taking, even on short articles
  4. Extract to vault - Readwise plugin pulls highlights into Obsidian, user connects them to existing notes

Counter-arguments

  • Aggressive curation may create filter bubbles and miss valuable serendipitous exposure
  • Treating every 5-minute read as a note-taking opportunity could make reading feel like work
  • The “cope” framing may unfairly dismiss the value of passive, exploratory consumption

Data gaps

  • No specifics on YB’s reading volume or how much note output this produces
  • “Structure your milieu” concept not ingested as a source
  • Single source describing this in an LLM-vault context
