A daily habit of jotting down observations, epiphanies, thoughts, and questions. Rooted in the historical tradition of commonplace books but adapted for LLM-augmented knowledge work.

In the context of a Claude + Obsidian workflow, the practice serves a dual purpose: personal reflection and building context for AI agents. The more raw personal material captured, the more an LLM can identify patterns and make connections.

Key principles (per YB):

  • Unstructured is fine - optimize for capture speed, not formatting
  • Voice transcription preferred - captures more including uncertainty markers
  • Context compounds - claimed benefits emerge after weeks of consistent practice
  • Personal, not synthetic - distinguishes this from llm-wiki-pattern research workflows where AI generates the artifacts

Counter-arguments

  • Unstructured notes may produce noise that degrades LLM context quality
  • The compounding claim rests on a single practitioner’s report
  • Historical commonplace books worked without AI - unclear what the LLM integration adds beyond search

Data gaps

  • No metrics on how much daily capture is needed for the compounding effect
  • No comparison with structured journaling approaches
  • Single source - needs corroboration
